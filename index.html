Process
1.) Discover
The first step to my design process is identifying the need or problem associated with the project. Asking the correct questions and understanding what should be asked is a large part of this step.
2.) Research
After understanding the need, I continue to research to find out a multitude of ways to attack the problem. I sketch lo-fi wireframes and build off of first ideas.
3.) Design
I move on to creating mid-fi wireframes after coming up with some initial designs. I try to create multiple versions of the wireframe before selecting fonts and colors to use on the mockups.
4.) Prototype
Once I have decided on which designs fit the user best, I prototype them. Connecting the dots in between each page while trying to guide the user with little explanation is my goal in this step always.
5.) Test
After selecting at least three final mockups, I will A/B test with the targeted user. This ensures that the designs work without explanation and lets the user decide what is best for them.
6.) Develop
I hand off the final design to developers, but my work does not end there. I can assist in the front end development portion of this process as well. I understand the developer's sense of functionality because I know what works.
7.) Launch and Repeat
Finally, when the development process is complete it is time to launch the final product. The UX Design process is never complete though. There will always be room to grow and create even better designs.
